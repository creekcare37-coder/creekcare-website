Professional Spring Yard Cleanups & Lawn Dethatching

After a long Canadian winter, lawns and garden beds are often covered in matted leaves, dead twigs, mold, and general debris. Getting your yard ready for the summer season takes significant work. Our hardworking student team at CreekCare provides comprehensive Spring Clean Up services in Findlay Creek and Blossom Park Ottawa to revive your property and kickstart healthy grass growth.

One of the most important parts of a spring cleanup is dethatching. Thatch is a layer of dead grass tissue, roots, and organic matter that sits between the green blades and the soil. A thick thatch layer blocks air, water, and nutrients from penetrating the soil. We carefully power-rake or hand-dethatch your lawn to remove this layer, then rake and bag all dead grass and debris, preparing your lawn for healthy summer growth.

What is Included:

  • Thorough raking and removal of winter leaf accumulation, twigs, and pinecones.
  • Lawn dethatching (power-raking) to remove dead thatch layers.
  • Edging and clearing garden beds of winter debris and old annuals.
  • Regular turf cleanup and bagging of yard waste for municipal collection.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is dethatching?

Dethatching removes the thick layer of dead grass, moss, and roots (thatch) that accumulates on your lawn over winter, allowing water, oxygen, and nutrients to reach the soil.

When should spring clean up be done?

Usually in late April or early May once the snow has fully melted and the ground has dried out enough to prevent soil compaction.

How much does a spring clean up cost?

Our student spring cleanup services start at $150, depending on your yard size and amount of winter debris. We provide free transparent estimates.
